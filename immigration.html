Immigration Psychological Evaluations

Navigating the immigration process can be one of the most challenging experiences in life. At Happy 2 Help Counseling, we provide professional, culturally sensitive immigration psychological evaluations to help support your case and strengthen your application.

Comprehensive Evaluations for Various Immigration Cases

We specialize in evaluations for:

  • Extreme Hardship Waivers (I-601 and I-601A)
  • Asylum Applications
  • U Visas for Victims of Crime
  • VAWA (Violence Against Women Act) Petitions
  • Cancellation of Removal Proceedings

Why Choose Happy 2 Help Counseling?

Expertise You Can Trust

Our owner and clinical director, Luis Ruiz, is a licensed Clinical Social Worker with years of experience conducting psychological evaluations for immigration cases. As an immigrant and U.S. Army veteran, he brings a unique understanding and deep empathy to every evaluation.

Culturally Sensitive, Trauma-Informed Care

Every person’s experience is unique. Our approach is culturally sensitive and trauma-informed, designed to create a safe, supportive environment where you can share your story.

Confidential & Professional

Your privacy is our priority. All evaluations are handled with professionalism and confidentiality, following the highest ethical standards.

What to Expect

  1. Initial Consultation: We’ll discuss your situation and what your immigration case requires.
  2. Comprehensive Evaluation: We’ll explore psychological history, current emotional functioning, and relevant experiences.
  3. Detailed Report: A professionally written report tailored to the evidentiary standards used by USCIS and immigration courts.
  4. Collaboration with Your Attorney: We can coordinate with your legal team to support the goals of your case.

Why These Evaluations Matter

In many immigration cases, psychological evaluations can make a meaningful difference. Reports can help explain how immigration-related stress, family separation, fear of return, or trauma impacts mental health. This provides courts and USCIS officials a clearer understanding of the human side of a case and can serve as compelling evidence in support of an application.
